Assembly details

Genome Reference Consortium GRCm38, which includes approximately 2.6 Gb of sequence, is considered to be "essentially complete". The assembly includes chromosomes 1-19, X, Y, M (mitochondrial DNA) and chr*_random (unlocalized) and chrUn_* (unplaced clone contigs). For information about the process used to assemble this version, see the GRC website. For a glossary of assembly-related terms, see the GRC Assembly Terminology page.

mm10 has been updated with patches since its release in 2012. GRC patch releases do not change any previously existing sequences; they simply add new sequences for fix patches or alternate haplotypes that correspond to specific regions of the main chromosome sequences. For most users, the patches are unlikely to make a difference and may complicate the analysis as they introduce more duplication.
